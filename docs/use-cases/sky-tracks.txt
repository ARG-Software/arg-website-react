SKY TRACKS

Industry: Music Tech
Client: SkyTracks (skytracks.io)
Timeline: 1 Year
Services: Frontend Migration, Latency Optimization, Instrument Connectivity, QA & Testing

THE CHALLENGE

SkyTracks is a browser-based music production platform — the kind of product where latency isn't a nice-to-have problem, it's a dealbreaker. Musicians can't create if there's a noticeable lag between hitting a note and hearing it. Earlier implementations left key features unreliable, the frontend was stuck on an outdated framework, and the product couldn't connect to external instruments. SkyTracks needed to ship a version that actually worked — for real musicians, in real creative workflows, across real devices.

WHAT ARG DELIVERED

ARG helped SkyTracks rebuild the product experience from the frontend down — modernizing the interface, cutting latency, and making the platform feel like a real production tool.

- Migrated the frontend to Angular and implemented the redesigned product interface from scratch
- Reduced data latency across interactive music workflows to make the platform genuinely responsive
- Connected external instruments so musicians could use hardware inputs inside the browser-based environment
- Stabilized features that were incomplete or unreliable in previous versions
- Reworked core music creation, editing, and playback flows for consistency across devices
- Supported QA and release work needed to launch the new platform version

THE RESULTS

SkyTracks launched a version that musicians can actually use. The platform now hosts over 50,000 online tracks, runs under 2,000ms average latency, and has powered over 1,000 collaborative sessions.

Key Metrics:
- Online tracks in production: 50,000+
- Average latency: <2,000ms
- Collaborative sessions: 1,000+

The platform moved from a promising concept to a working product — one that's fast enough for creative flow, stable enough for real use, and open enough to connect to the instruments musicians already own.

TECHNOLOGIES USED

Knex, Tone.js, Tailwind, Koa, Angular, Docker, Jest, Node
